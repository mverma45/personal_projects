Structure of the Game

Basketball is a team sport, each team has 12-15 players, 5 players from each team play at one time againist each other. The tallest player on each team is usually the center (C), the second tallest and the strongest is the power forward (PF), a slightly shorter player is the small forward (SF) the shortest players the best ball handlers are the point guard (PG) and the shooting guard (SG) The game is played on a rectangular court, a basket is mounted 10 feet high with a backboard at each end of the court. Each team defends their own basket so the opposing player doesn't score hench giving them points. There are 3 officals or referees who watch the game closely and call any fouls on the court. A game has 4 12 minute quarters which makes the game 48 minutes long. (NBA) A college game is 2 halves consisting of 20 minute halves making it 40 minutes long.

A player advances a ball by bouncing it, while walking or running, they can pass it to a teammate The players on the offense can shoot the ball in the basket with a jumpshot, a layup a dunk. The defense can steal the ball, intercept the ball, or block the shot if a shot is missed any player is allowed to grab the ball making it a rebound. You may not drag a pivot foot without dribbling the basketball carry it, or hold the ball with both hands and then dribbling the ball.

Scoring

Each field goal or (FG) is 2 points, if a shot is made behind a line that basket is 3 points if contact is made with the player shooting and the player defending a foul is called the game clock stops and the player is awarded 2 free shots if he was shooting a 3 point shot he gets awarded 3 free shots. If the player made the shot and gets fouled he get awarded 1 free shot at the free throw line which is 15 feet from the basket. The team with the most points at the end of regulation wins the game, if the score is tied overtime (OT) which is 5 minutes each. OT is required until their is one winner.
